Research Focus

Our group combines synthetic methodology with biological application to solve complex problems in molecular sciences.

Bio-inspired synthesis of terpenoids

We focus on the semi-synthetic preparation of rearranged terpenoids with interesting biological activity. Our strategy involves mimicking natural biosynthesis pathways, starting from commercially available natural precursors to access complex molecular architectures efficiently.

Photoaffinity probes & MCRs

In collaboration with Solgate GmbH, we are designing fragment-based photoaffinity probes. We develop novel methods to synthesize small molecules containing photoactive functional groups (diazirines), employing high-throughput synthesis to create libraries for biological evaluation.

Principal Investigator

Dr. Nicolas Kratena is currently a Principal Investigator at TU Wien, leading research projects in bio-inspired synthesis and medicinal chemistry. With a strong foundation in organic synthesis from his education at TU Wien and postdoctoral research at the University of Oxford, his group tackles challenges at the interface of chemistry and biology.
